

A revolving door is convenient for two-direction travel, but it also serves as a security measure at a...

A bank? No, there are no security measures needed at banks.

A library? No, there are no security measures needed at libraries either.

A department store? No, there are no security measures needed at department stores either.

A mall? No, there are no security measures needed at malls either.

E New York? No, there are no security measures needed in New York either.

So, the answer is...

C A DEPARTMENT STORE!

That's right! A revolving door is a common feature in department stores, and it serves as a security measure to prevent shoplifting and other forms of theft. The rotating door makes it difficult for customers to exit the store without passing through the checkout area, which helps to deter theft.
